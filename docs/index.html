Hello,

My name is Charlie Smart. I’m a graphics editor at the New York Times. I write software and design graphics to communicate data in the news. Here are some projects I’m proud of.

At the Times, I helped design and build our Covid-19 case tracking pages and hospitalizations tracker . Both were included in a package that won the 2021 Pulitzer Prize for public service journalism.

I developed an interactive game to explain the mechanics of gerrymandering and built interactive graphics on a number of topics, including elections , campaign finance and more .

I also helped build our 2020 election live forecast and created live zoomable Dorling cartograms for the primaries.

I worked with Salesforce and The Pudding to train a text classifier to sort every tweet by members of Congress into policy topics and built a web app to explore that data.

I contributed an interactive data visualization to YouTube’s 2019 YouTube Rewind .

At Fathom Information Design, I helped build an interactive web app to explore themes in the archive of the podcast “On Being” and a ProPublica database to explore government money spent at Trump properties.
